Reviewer 1

1. Significance
Strengths:
- The proposal addresses a critical gap in pancreatic cancer modeling.
- Strong clinical relevance.

Weaknesses:
- The rationale for choosing CPA concentrations is insufficiently justified. The authors should provide dose-response data.
- The claim that "2D systems cannot model tumor heterogeneity" is an oversimplification. Recent microfluidic approaches have shown promising results.

2. Investigator(s)
Strengths:
- PI has strong track record in bioprinting.

Weaknesses:
- No co-investigator with cryobiology expertise is listed. This is a significant gap given the centrality of cryopreservation to the project.

3. Innovation
Strengths:
- Cryobioprinting is a novel concept.

Weaknesses:
- The novelty claim is overstated. Similar in-situ freezing approaches have been published by Zhang et al. (2022). The authors must clearly differentiate their method.
- Lack of comparison with existing vitrification methods for 3D bioprinted tissues.

4. Approach
Strengths:
- Well-structured experimental design.

Weaknesses:
- The statistical analysis plan is vague. Sample sizes are not justified with power analysis.
- No mention of how batch-to-batch variability in bioink composition will be controlled.
- The timeline for completing Aim 2 is unrealistic given the complexity of high-throughput optimization.

Reviewer 2

1. Significance
Strengths:
- Important problem in cancer therapeutics.

Weaknesses:
- The proposal does not adequately address how the model will be validated against patient-derived xenograft (PDX) models.

2. Innovation
Strengths:
- Fresh approach to tissue preservation.

Weaknesses:
- The authors claim their bioink is "unique" but do not compare it to commercially available bioinks. This weakens the innovation argument.

3. Approach
Strengths:
- Good pilot data.

Weaknesses:
- Aim 1 lacks a clear milestone for determining success vs failure of the cryopreservation protocol.
- The transition from Aim 1 to Aim 2 is not well-defined. What criteria determine readiness to proceed?
